### PriceBasket Data Reveals: Quick Commerce Apps Charge Up to 32% More During Peak Hours

**Mumbai, [DATE]** — A new analysis by PriceBasket.in, India's grocery price comparison platform, has found that prices on major quick-commerce apps including Blinkit, Zepto, and Instamart fluctuate by as much as 32% based on time of day, day of week, and user location — a practice that costs Indian consumers an estimated ₹4,200 crore annually in avoidable overpayments.

The study, which analysed over 2.3 million price data points collected over 90 days across 6 platforms and 12 Indian cities, found:

**Key Findings:**

1. **Peak-hour pricing**: Prices on quick-commerce apps are 12-18% higher between 7-9pm (dinner rush) compared to 11am-1pm on the same day for identical products.

2. **Weekend premium**: Grocery prices are on average 8.4% higher on Saturday and Sunday compared to Tuesday-Thursday.

3. **Location-based pricing**: The same product in a Tier-1 neighbourhood costs 14-22% more than in a Tier-2 neighbourhood within the same city.

4. **Platform loyalty tax**: Users who exclusively use one platform pay 23% more annually than users who compare prices before purchasing.

5. **Most volatile categories**: Fruits & vegetables (±34%), dairy (±28%), and cooking oils (±24%) show the highest price volatility.

**The Cities Most Affected:**
- Mumbai: Average overpayment ₹1,240/month
- Delhi NCR: Average overpayment ₹1,180/month  
- Bengaluru: Average overpayment ₹980/month
- Hyderabad: Average overpayment ₹870/month
- Chennai: Average overpayment ₹760/month

"Dynamic pricing in grocery is a silent tax on Indian families," said [Founder Name], Co-founder of PriceBasket.in. "Unlike flight tickets where consumers expect price changes, most people assume grocery prices are fixed. Our data shows they're not — and the difference can be hundreds of rupees on a single order."

**Methodology**

PriceBasket.in's automated price tracking system collects prices every 15 minutes across all major platforms. The analysis covered January-March 2025, across Mumbai, Delhi, Bengaluru, Hyderabad, Chennai, Pune, Ahmedabad, Kolkata, Jaipur, Lucknow, Chandigarh, and Kochi.
